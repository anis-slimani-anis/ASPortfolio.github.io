EduSphere

Augmented Reality for Engaging Education

Course & Academic Context

EduSphere was first developed during the Mixed Reality and Tangible Interfaces course at Université Paris-Saclay, focusing on interactive and immersive learning experiences. Later, a user pilot A/B study with teachers was conducted as part of the Design Project 2 course to evaluate its effectiveness in real-world educational settings.

Sector & Relevance

This project is part of the Educational Technology (EdTech) and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) sectors, particularly in augmented reality for learning. It addresses the challenge of keeping children engaged in educational content by integrating multi-sensory interaction, active learning, and gamified experiences.

Project Overview

EduSphere is an AR-based educational tool designed to teach children about dinosaurs through interactive flashcards. By scanning a physical card, learners can view 3D animated dinosaurs, hear their sounds, and explore their size, habitat, and characteristics.

Key features:

  • Edu Mode – Displays 3D dinosaurs with interactive facts and sound effects.
  • Quiz Mode – Users answer trivia questions by placing AR markers, receiving immediate feedback.
  • Paleontology Mode – Simulates a fossil excavation using a tangible brush tool.

The goal is to make learning interactive and memorable, moving beyond traditional textbook-based education.

Development & Research Process

1. Prototype and Initial Development (Mixed Reality Course)

  • Implemented with Unity 2022.3 and Vuforia 10.18 for AR tracking.
  • Designed flashcard interactions to blend tangible and digital learning.
  • Focused on active learning, allowing children to experiment and explore concepts dynamically.

2. User Testing and A/B Pilot Study (Design Project 2)

  • Conducted comparative testing between traditional paper-based learning and AR-based EduSphere.
  • Six educators participated, testing both versions and sharing qualitative feedback.
  • Evaluated engagement, information retention, and user experience through questionnaires and interviews.

Challenges & Key Insights

• Technical Complexity of AR Tracking

Ensuring flashcards were recognizable yet visually appealing was a challenge. The size and design of markers needed optimization for both readability and usability.

• Balancing Engagement and Educational Value

While AR animations improved attention and motivation, some users focused more on visuals than learning objectives. Refining content delivery to keep the focus on education was crucial.

• Educators' Perspective on AR in Classrooms

Teachers viewed EduSphere as a valuable supplementary tool, not a replacement for traditional methods. Customization for different learning levels was highlighted as a potential future improvement.

Professional Impact & Learnings

This project demonstrates expertise in:

  • Immersive and Tangible Interfaces – Designing interactive AR experiences that blend physical and digital learning.
  • UX Research and Evaluation – Conducting user studies with educators, applying A/B testing methodologies.
  • Educational Technology and Gamification – Creating engaging, knowledge-driven interactions for young learners.

EduSphere is an example of how AR can enhance primary education, making learning more engaging, interactive, and accessible.
