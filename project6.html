CHOREOSPHERE

A Virtual Reality Choreography Tool for Gymnastics

Course & Academic Context

This project was developed as part of the Advanced Immersive Interaction course, focusing on VR interaction design and real-time motion visualization. The goal was to create a virtual reality tool for gymnasts to choreograph routines more efficiently while reducing physical strain.

Sector & Relevance

CHOREOSPHERE falls within Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Sports Technology, and Virtual Reality (VR), addressing key challenges in gymnastics choreography, injury prevention, and training efficiency. The system allows athletes to visualize, refine, and score their routines in VR before physically practicing them.

Project Overview

CHOREOSPHERE is a VR-based gymnastics choreography tool designed to streamline routine planning by allowing athletes to:

  • Preview and refine routines in a 3D space without excessive physical repetition.
  • Sequence jumps and movements on a VR timeline, ensuring transitions meet competition standards.
  • Receive real-time scoring based on difficulty and execution criteria.
  • Reduce injury risks by eliminating unnecessary trial-and-error in early-stage choreography.

Key Features:

  • VR timeline system – Enables gymnasts to sequence and modify their routine in an intuitive 3D interface.
  • Predefined gymnastics animations – Users can select, reorder, and test jumps for optimal choreography.
  • Real-time visualization – A third-person view allows gymnasts to observe their routine from different angles.
  • Scoring system – Assigns difficulty scores to each movement, helping athletes optimize their final routine.
  • Music synchronization – Background music integrates with the timeline to ensure rhythmic precision.

Research & Development Process

1. Identifying Choreography Challenges

  • Gymnasts face physical strain from repetitive trial-and-error adjustments.
  • Existing choreography methods rely on video recordings, which lack interactive real-time feedback.

2. Prototyping & Design

  • Figma prototypes were developed to define UI layout, visual hierarchy, and VR interface placement.
  • Transitioned from a 2D timeline to a fully interactive 3D timeline for improved usability in VR.
  • Unity and the XR Interaction Toolkit were used to develop VR interactions and ensure seamless movement visualization.

3. Implementation & Testing

  • Created a VR environment optimized for choreographic design, allowing gymnasts to manipulate movement sequences.
  • Developed direct manipulation interactions for timeline sequencing, where users can grab and reposition jumps easily.
  • Addressed challenges in animation transitions to ensure smooth, natural movement flow.

Challenges & Key Insights

• Transitioning from 2D to 3D UI in VR

Moving from a flat interface to a spatial VR timeline required rethinking interaction mechanics to ensure precision and usability.

• Ensuring Smooth Motion Transitions

Early prototypes had rigid animations that disrupted the flow of movements. Refining predefined animations and transition effects improved natural movement sequences.

• Balancing User Control and Automation

Some gymnasts preferred manual control over every move, while others wanted automated suggestions. A balance was achieved by allowing both predefined animations and custom choreography adjustments.

• Music Synchronization Challenges

Integrating background music with real-time movement adjustments required fine-tuning playback consistency within Unity's timeline system.

Professional Impact & Learnings

This project demonstrates expertise in:

  • Virtual Reality Interaction Design – developing 3D spatial interfaces for real-time motion planning.
  • Human-Computer Interaction in Sports – optimizing UX for athletes to improve choreography efficiency.
  • Game Development & Motion Integration – implementing animation pipelines and interactive movement sequencing.

CHOREOSPHERE reimagines gymnastics training and choreography, reducing physical strain while enhancing creative freedom. The system has potential applications beyond gymnastics, including dance, acrobatics, and performing arts.
