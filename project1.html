Circles

A Redesign of Instagram

Course & Academic Context

This project was developed for the Advanced Design of Interactive Things course at Université Paris-Saclay. The course explores innovative interaction design, user research, and iterative prototyping to create interactive systems that improve user experience.

Sector & Relevance

Circles falls within the Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and UX/UI Design sectors, addressing key issues in digital well-being, online social interactions, and engagement design. The project responds to concerns about addictive social media patterns, privacy, and the quality of online connections.

Project Overview

Circles is a redesigned social networking platform aimed at fostering more meaningful interactions while reducing digital overconsumption. It structures engagement into four layers:

  • Self Circle – Personal screen-time management.
  • Inner Circle – Private, close relationships.
  • Outer Circle – Broader passive social interactions.
  • Discovery Circle – Meeting new people and organizing events.

This approach gives users more control over their digital interactions, encouraging balanced and intentional social media use.

Design Process

The project followed a user-centered design methodology, combining research, ideation, prototyping, and testing:

1. Understanding User Needs

  • Conducted user interviews and surveys to identify frustrations with existing social media.
  • Analyzed privacy concerns, engagement patterns, and social habits.

2. Ideation during Participatory Workshops

  • Mapped ideas using brainstorming workshops and post-it schematic analysis.
  • Refined interaction models using The Funnel Method, filtering out ineffective concepts.

3. Prototyping & Testing

  • Created paper prototypes for initial usability testing.
  • Developed a high-fidelity interactive video prototype to simulate real usage.
  • Conducted live user tests to evaluate engagement and interaction flow.

Challenges & Key Insights

• Balancing Privacy & Socialization

Users wanted privacy but also easy connections. We designed customizable visibility settings that adapt dynamically.

• Reducing Addictive Patterns While Keeping Engagement

By removing likes and follower counts, we shifted the focus from passive scrolling to active interaction.

• Encouraging Real-World Interactions

The Discovery Circle feature bridges digital and real-life connections, making social media less isolating.

Professional Impact & Learnings

This project demonstrates expertise in:

  • UX/UI & Interaction Design – Creating intuitive and ethical digital interactions.
  • User Research & Testing – Using real user data to inform design decisions.
  • Prototyping & Iteration – Rapidly refining concepts based on feedback.
  • Team Collaboration – Working in a multidisciplinary setting to solve complex problems.

Circles highlights the need for responsible digital design, proving that social platforms can be engaging without being addictive.
